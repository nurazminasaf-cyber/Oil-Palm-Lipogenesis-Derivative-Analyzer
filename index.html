The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysia, optimizing the harvest cycle of Oil Palm is critical for maximizing Oil Extraction Rate (OER). During fruit development, lipids accumulate in the mesocarp (pulp) cell tissue. This lipogenesis is slow at first, accelerates rapidly, and then plateaus as the fruit reaches absolute ripeness.

Why Derivatives Matter:

While the absolute oil content $f(x)$ tells us the current oil yield, the first derivative $f'(x)$ (Rate of Oil Synthesis) pinpoints the precise metabolic peak of the plant. If we harvest during peak synthesis rate, we miss potential yield. Harvesting when the rate drops to zero signals maximum cumulative yield—but waiting too long triggers cellular breakdown, rising Free Fatty Acids (FFA), and acidic contamination of the crude palm oil (CPO).

Mathematical Model (Logistic Growth)

We model the cumulative oil percentage of the dry mesocarp $f(x)$ over time $x$ (in Weeks After Anthesis, WAA) using a generalized logistic function:

Cumulative Oil Content (% Dry Base):

$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$

Accumulation Rate (Derivative):

$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}$$

Parameters based on Malaysian Field Data:

$L = 80\% $ Max Oil Capacity
$k = 0.8$ Synthesis Coeff.
$x_0 = 17 \text{ WAA}$ Peak Synthesis Pt.
